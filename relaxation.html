What are relaxation techniques?

Relaxation techniques such as breathing exercises, grounding exercises and progressive muscle relaxation can calm the mind and ground you to prevent the stream of anxious thoughts

Taking 6 deep breaths within 30 seconds can significantly reduce blood pressure*

Box Breathing Technique

Follow a simple 4 step routine repeating until you feel calm.

  1. Visualize a box
  2. Imagine tracing up the box, inhale for 4 seconds as you trace up
  3. Trace across the box, holding the breath for 4 seconds
  4. Trace down the box, exhaling for 4 seconds
  5. Complete the box by tracing across to the start, hold the empty breath for 4 seconds

4-7-8 Breathing Technique

Follow a simple routine repeating until you feel calm.

  1. Inhale through the nose with the mouth closed and tongue resting behind the front teeth for 4 seconds
  2. Hold the breath for 7 seconds
  3. Exhale through the mouth for 8 seconds making a whooshing sound

5,4,3,2,1 Grounding Exercise

Ease state of mind through practicing this grounding technique using your senses following these prompts

  1. Name 5 things you can see
  2. Name 4 things you can touch
  3. Name 3 things you can hear
  4. Name 2 things you can smell
  5. Name 1 thing you can taste

Progressive Muscle Relaxation

Progressive muscle relaxation (PMR) is a relaxation technique that reduces stress and physical tension by systematically tensing and then releasing groups of muscles throughout the body. Follow along this guide

  • Focus on different muscle groups such as the arms, legs, face, hand, core
  1. Tense one muscle group for 5 seconds while inhaling, hold for 5 seconds
  2. Suddenly release and relax the muscle for 10 seconds while exhaling
  3. Repeat throughout different muscle groups
