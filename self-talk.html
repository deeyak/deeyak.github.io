What is Positive Self Talk?

Positive self-talk is repeating affirmations to yourself. This can shift your inner dialogue and mind to embody these affirmations and what you are saying. Positive self-talk makes you feel good about yourself and can boost confidence to reduce anxiety.

  • Positive self talk can improve performance by up to 11%*

General Examples:

  • "I have the skills needed to perform well"
  • "I enjoy feeling challenged by competition"
  • "I can stay focused under pressure"
  • "I am a skilled athlete"

Personalized Examples:

  • "I scored the winning goal last game"
  • "I ran my fastest time at practice the other day, I can do it again"
  • "I had a really good passes in warm up"

*https://www.positiveperformancetraining.com/blog/the-truth-about-positive-self-talk

Positive Self Talk by Sport

Here are some examples of Self-Talk based on specific sport played

Soccer

"All my passes will go where I want them to go."

"I will block out every cross coming in."

"My shots have been good in practice. I can do that again."

Basketball

"I will make a 3 pointer."

"I am confident I will win every rebound."

"I am strong enough to box out the other players."

Volleyball

"I am skilled at blocking hits."

"I had good sets in warm-ups."

"I have the resources to handle the pressure"

Track/XC

"I hit a PR in practice, I can do that again."

"My coaches and teammates cheering me on will make me do good and run faster."

"I enjoy feeling accomplished after a race."

Softball

"I have been practicing the skills and drills needed to succeed."

"I saved the game winning point last game."

"I enjoy playing with my teammates and attacking."

Tennis

"My serves have been good lately."

"I have the stamina to get to each ball."

"I have trust in my doubles partner."

Swimming

"I like the feeling of being in the water."

"I am doing a stroke I feel comfortable with."

"My teammates will be cheering me on."

Dance/Gymnastics

"I scored high at my last meet."

"I will have good turns because I have the ability to be able to."

"My last performances were good."
