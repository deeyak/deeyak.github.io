Introduction to Sports Anxiety

What is Sports Anxiety?

Sports anxiety is the habit of viewing competitive situations such as games or competitions as threatening or frightful causing apprehension and tension when engaging in the activity.

Common Causes:

  • Performance Stressors: Stress in regards to how prepared you feel, the thoughts of getting injured, and self-set expectations you have on your performance
  • Organizational Stressors = Stress in regards to the competitive environment, your role in the team, coaches and team expectations of you

Symptoms:

  • Tremors
  • Racing Heart
  • Hyperventilation
  • Muscle Tension
  • Bathroom Troubles
  • Sweating
  • Self doubt and Negativity

How Does Sports Anxiety Impact My Performance?

A little bit of anxiety before competitions can be helpful as it prepares us to meet the demands of the stress presented and encourage us to be more motivated. However, anxiety at increased levels can be harmful in sport competition. Higher levels of anxiety can cause decreased sport performance and increase the risk of injury due to stress increasing muscle tension, narrowing attention and causing coordination difficulties.

Breaking the Stigma Around Sports Anxiety

If you are experiencing anxiety in sports, you are not alone. According to the American College of Sports Medicine, 30% of women and 25% of men student-athletes report experiencing anxiety. Furthermore, performance anxiety affects 30–60% of athletes*. Olympians and high-level athletes, such as Simone Biles, have also spoken publicly about experiencing sports anxiety*. Experiencing anxiety before competition does not mean you are weak or underprepared. Anxiety can affect athletes at every level, and it is okay to seek help and support.
